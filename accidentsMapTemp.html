Latest Rules/Regulations and Technologies

  • Feb 2017

    The Integrated Traffic Management System recently suggested the use of "Speed Indication Signs". These are placed at road where the chances of road accidents are much higher, determines the speed of vehicles with the help of radar sensor and to check whether the vehicle is in violation of over speeding or not. Many of the developed countries has already put this idea into use. But in India, it is just at its budding stage. Read More...

  • Feb 2017

    To avoid jams, Delhi Development Authority authorised Variable message signs to be put at use. These are digital boards which are used to relay messages to the commuters in the real time. They can relay messages like any barricades up ahead the lane etc. Cities like Hyderabad and Bengaluru have been using them since 2014. Delhi became a part of this when the message signs were launched in March 2017. Sardar Patel Marg, IGI Airport Road etc. were the victims to high traffic jams and to keep everything under control, these signs are used within every 50 kms or before every flyover. Read More...

  • Feb 2017

    Ministry of Road Transport and Highways(MORTH) released a bunch of policies which are going to serve as a step towards putting road accidents to a halt. Anti-lock Braking system(ABS) are now mandatory in Heavy vehicles. ABS and Automatic Headlights On (AHO) are also going to be mandatory in two wheelers w.e.f 1st April 2018. Cars must have seat provision for at least 1 child. Read More...

  • Jan 2017

    Ministry of Road Transportation and Highways(MORTH) made it official on 31st January 2017 that all commercial vehicles must be equipped with speed governors. Speed Governors are devices which put an upper limit on the vehicle speed which is far less than the actual maximum speed of the vehicle. This way even if someone tries to cross the upper limit, it is impossible to actually do that. It limits the amount of air and fuel receiving to the engine when the speed is near the apparent maximum speed, putting a halt to increase in the speed. This initiative will save hundreds of lives during any case of mishappening. Read More...

IRSC

The Indian Road Safety Campaign was started as a project under the IIT Delhi chapter of National Service Scheme with help of Transport Research and Injury Prevention Program ( set up under Government of India) when few students of IIT Delhi lost their precious life in a road accident just after being placed in well reputed firms. This incidence shook the student community from within and the students and faculty decided that we will make sure their death doesnt go in vain and we make sure that no lives are further lost to road-accidents. Hence a small team started this project in IIT Delhi level and slowly and gradually we have become a youth-led mission being led by students from various colleges across India. All of us just have one aim, to make Indian roads safer by working on all aspects of road safety! Indian Road Safety Campaign, is currently registered as the road safety project of Solve, a not-for profit trust formed by students and the alumni of IIT Delhi to "solve" the problems plaguing the Indian ecosystem on ground level. Solve believes in creating ground level impact by promoting volunteerism in youth and using technology to solve the problems.
